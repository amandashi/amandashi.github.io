WHO I AM

I'm a sophomore at Princeton University interested in computer science, economics, & psychology. I direct Princeton's Social Impact Competition, organize HackPrinceton, & journal at The Huffington Post & Medium.

This summer, I worked in product & web development at Ashoka, a social impact investing organization with a global network of over 3,000 fellows.

WHAT I MAKE

I'm passionate about building tools for inclusion, equality, & transparency.

Ashoka Whisper - Built with HTML, CSS, & Javascript using Meteor
Built Ashoka Whisper in response to observed lack of transparency at Ashoka. Whisper broacasts employee sentiments to catalyze discussions & solutions on issues ranging from unequal pay to systematic biases in the hiring process. For internal use.
GirlCode - Built with Objective C using Xcode
Intuitive way to search for women’s bathroom codes on Princeton University’s campus to fight institutionalized gender norms & inconvenience; lead to controversy & increased campus dialogue. Although eventually shut down by the administration, GirlCode remains a recollection of a better (albeit brief) time when females had equal bathroom privileges as males, & a reminder to Princeton that we can do better.
Bioengineering & Materials Science Research
During the summer of 2013, I developed a novel bio-compatible scaffold using polymers & nanoparticles that regenerates lost dental tissues following trauma to the tooth. I was a 2013 Siemens Competition Semifinalist (top 300 projects in the nation). Check out my full research paper here.
